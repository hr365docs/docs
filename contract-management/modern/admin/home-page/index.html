    Home Page

    CLM365's Home Page offers a centralized interface for quick access to contracts, requests, tasks, and approvals. It provides real-time insights through dashboards and widgets, streamlining contract lifecycle management from a single view.


  • Draft: This section would contain requests that have been created but are saved as drafts, meaning they are not yet submitted
  • Request: This section displays created and submitted requests.
  • Contract: Shows active contracts that are currently in the approval process.
  • Expiring Soon: Displays contracts that are nearing their expiration date.
  • Expired: Lists contracts that have already expired, identified as those with dates earlier than the current date.
  • Terminated: Displays contracts that have been terminated.
  • My Task: Shows tasks that are pending approval by the task owner.

    Deviation

    A deviation refers to any modification or exception made to the standard terms, clauses, or policies defined in a contract template. These deviations typically arise during drafting, negotiation, or approval phases and require careful review to minimize risks. For instance, a deviation might involve altering payment terms, such as modifying the schedule or amounts.
    In our application, the Deviation Task feature is designed to manage these exceptions efficiently. A Deviation Task can be created directly from the timeline when a request is in the legal review stage. While approving the legal review, a deviation task can also be initiated from the timeline. Once created, the task facilitates communication between the creator and the assigned approver, enabling clarifications or updates.
    Approvers can review the task details and either approve or reject it. All deviation tasks associated with a request can be easily tracked, ensuring transparency, as shown in the figures below.
    To create a deviation task, click on "Create Deviation" from the timeline as demonstrated below.


    When "Create Deviation" is clicked, the dialog shown below will open. Users need to fill in the deviation name, assigned person, due date, and provide a necessary description.


    Once the task is created, it will appear in the "My Tasks" tab in the application. The image below illustrates how a deviation task can be approved or rejected. Additionally, the approver can reply to the creator for further clarification.



    After a deviation task is approved, the task status will be reflected under the "Deviation" tab of the corresponding request, as shown below.


    If multiple deviations are created for the same request, all deviations along with their statuses can be viewed in the "Recent Deviations" section, as shown below.


    Risk and Mitigation

    The Risk and Mitigation feature provides a robust solution to manage risks and implement effective mitigation strategies. To use this feature, AI must be enabled in the settings. Once enabled, the Risk section becomes accessible, allowing users to identify and assess potential issues in uploaded documents. By clicking on the Risk section, the AI generates detailed insights, including risk type, related clause, severity, priority, score, and status, as shown in the figure below. This analysis occurs during the initial review of a request or contract, and subsequent reviews retrieve these details from the backend for consistency and efficiency.


    Editing and Customizing Risks
    Users can edit AI-generated risk details, such as severity, priority, score, and status, as shown in the figure below. Remarks can also be added for specific risks, which can be assigned to any user. AI-generated risks are marked as "Identified by AI." If a risk's status changes to "Mitigated," it moves to the Mitigated pivot.


    Adding Risks Manually
    Users can manually identify risks from the contract and add them to the Risk pivot by clicking the "Add" button. Risks added manually display the creator's name in the detail list.


    Mitigation Process
    Mitigation is a crucial step in resolving identified risks. In the edit panel of a non-mitigated risk, an eye icon appears, as shown below.


    Clicking the icon opens a file editor, displaying the risk and its associated clause on the right-hand side, along with a "Search Alternative Clauses" button. The headings "Risk Clause" and "All" are clickable. Clicking "All" displays a list of all risks for the request, along with their associated clauses. The "Search Alternative Clauses" button appears for any clause.



    Clicking the "Search Alternative Clauses" button opens a panel where the AI generates alternative clauses relevant to the chosen clause. These alternatives appear in the "Alternative Clauses by AI" section. Selecting an alternative clause opens a text field for editing before incorporating it into the document.


    Alternative Clauses from Repository
    Below the "Alternative Clauses by AI" section, users can access the "Alternative Clauses from Clause Library" section by clicking a dropdown arrow.


    Clicking the arrow opens a pop-up, allowing users to select clause types and tags to fetch relevant alternatives from the clause library. The selected clause can then be integrated into the document.


    After selecting the desired content, users can seamlessly integrate it into the document by clicking "Update in Contract," as shown below.


    Once mitigation content is added and finalized, the associated risk's status updates to "Mitigated." This change reflects that the issue has been resolved and appears in the Mitigated pivot, as shown below.

    Review and Consult

    The Review and Consult feature allows users to initiate a review or consultation process for a specific file along with its contract type. This process can be initiated in two ways: either with a contract type or without one. If a contract type is involved, it must first be enabled in the application's general settings, as shown in the image below.


    Users can create a review or consult task from the Request tab, as illustrated below. Here, users are required to provide the necessary details to initiate the process.


    Upon clicking "Review" or "Consult," the following panel opens. If the contract type is enabled, users need to fill in the required fields, as shown in the image below. Once the details are completed, click "Submit" to initiate the task. The review or consult task will appear in "My Tasks" for the assigned user.


    Additional Review Workflow Levels

    Users can create additional reviews for tasks that have been created for approval. On the approval page, the “Request Additional Review” button is available. This option allows tasks requiring further review and approval from another person. Users can create additional reviews in a sequence, meaning tasks are approved one after another by additional reviewers, or they can simply create additional tasks without sequencing.


    When the “Request Additional Review” button is clicked, the dialog shown below opens. Users must provide the additional reviewer’s name, remarks, and due date. Attachments can also be added if necessary.


    After clicking “Next,” users can create additional reviews in a flow, as shown below. Levels can be added as necessary, or users can proceed without creating levels. Once all required details are completed, click “Submit” to create the additional review task. These tasks are assigned and appear as follows:


    If additional review levels are created, the corresponding tasks are generated when the user completes the current review. The process continues for as many additional levels as the user has defined.


    Tags in Contract Summary

    The AI Summary provides a detailed overview of contract summaries, obligations, and associated tags, including Risk, Law, and Tax counts. These tags play a crucial role in categorizing contract-related elements, helping users quickly assess key aspects of their agreements:

    • Risk: Indicates clauses or provisions that may pose potential risks, allowing users to take proactive measures.
    • Law: Reflects legal references within the contract, ensuring compliance with regulatory obligations.
    • Tax: Highlights tax-related clauses, assisting in financial and statutory planning.

    To enable these tags in the AI Summary, users must activate AI in the settings with the appropriate credentials. This allows for automated analysis and extraction of essential details.

    Draft

    The Draft File Comparison feature allows users to view the differences between the first uploaded draft and the final draft of a document. This feature is especially useful in tracking revisions in contracts, policy documents, or any collaborative files.

    To access this feature:

    1. Navigate to the Drafts section of the contract or document.
    2. Click on the option "Compare with First Draft".


    3. A comparison panel will appear, showing the first and final versions along with highlighted changes.

    This feature enhances document control and provides stakeholders with visibility into how content has evolved throughout the drafting process.
