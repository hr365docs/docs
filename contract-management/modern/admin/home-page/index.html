    Deviation

    In general, a deviation refers to any change or an exception made to the standard terms, clauses, or policies predefined in a contract template. These deviations can arise during the drafting, negotiation, or approval stages of a contract and often require careful review to ensure there are any unnecessary risks. For example, a deviation could involve modifying payment terms, such as changing the schedule or amounts.
    In our application, we have this feature called the Deviation Task, which is specifically designed to manage these exceptions efficiently. A Deviation Task can be created directly from the timeline whenever a request is in the legal review stage. While approving the legal review, a deviation Task can also be created from the timeline. Once a deviation Task is created, it facilitates communication between the task creator and the assigned approver, allowing them to respond to each other for further clarifications or updates.
    The approver has the authority to either approve or reject the task after reviewing its details. Additionally, all deviation tasks associated with a specific request can be easily viewed, ensuring transparency as demonstrated in the figures below.

    Review

    The Review Contract section provides detailed information on the contract review process within the system. This feature allows users to review, track, and manage contract documents effectively. It ensures that contracts go through a structured review and approval process before finalization.


    The New Request form allows users to submit a contract for review by selecting a recipient responsible for the review, setting the urgency level (e.g., High), attaching supporting documents, and finalizing the request by clicking the Submit button for processing.


    Consult

    The Consult Contract section provides detailed information on the contract consultation process within the system. This feature allows users to review, discuss, and seek expert opinions on contract documents before finalization. It ensures that contracts undergo a structured consultation process, allowing stakeholders to collaborate, provide insights, and make informed decisions.


    The New Request form for Consultation allows users to submit a contract for expert advice by selecting a recipient, defining the priority level (e.g., High), attaching relevant documents, and finalizing the request by clicking the Submit button for processing.
