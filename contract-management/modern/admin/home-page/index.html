    AI Summary

    The "AI Summary" section is intended to provide an automatically generated summary of the document. However, in this case, the system was unable to generate a summary because the document did not contain any text to analyze. If a different document with text is provided, the AI can generate a relevant summary based on its contents.
    If the content is more than 100 to 200 pages, the AI Summary will focus on key aspects such as an overview, major sections, important terms, risk and compliance factors, actionable insights, and key takeaways to provide a concise yet comprehensive understanding of the document.



    Deviation

    In general, a deviation refers to any change or an exception made to the standard terms, clauses, or policies predefined in a contract template. These deviations can arise during the drafting, negotiation, or approval stages of a contract and often require careful review to ensure there are any unnecessary risks. For example, a deviation could involve modifying payment terms, such as changing the schedule or amounts.
    In our application, we have this feature called the Deviation Task, which is specifically designed to manage these exceptions efficiently. A Deviation Task can be created directly from the timeline whenever a request is in the legal review stage. While approving the legal review, a deviation Task can also be created from the timeline. Once a deviation Task is created, it facilitates communication between the task creator and the assigned approver, allowing them to respond to each other for further clarifications or updates.
    The approver has the authority to either approve or reject the task after reviewing its details. Additionally, all deviation tasks associated with a specific request can be easily viewed, ensuring transparency as demonstrated in the figures below.

    Risk and Mitigation

    Risk and Mitigation offers a comprehensive feature to manage risks and implement effective mitigations. To use this feature AI must be enabled in the settings, once enabled the Risk section becomes visible, allowing users to identify and assess potential issues within document uploaded. By clicking on the Risk section, the AI generates detailed information, including the risk type, related clause, severity, priority, score, and status as shown in below figure. This detailed analysis is performed during the initial review of a request or contract. For subsequent reviews of the same document, these details are retrieved from backend so that it saves users time and consistency.

    Tags in Contract summary

    Ai Summary provides a detailed overview of contract summary, obligations and associated tags, tags include Risk, Law, and Tax counts. These tags play a crucial role in categorizing contract related elements, helping users quickly assess key aspects of their agreements. The Risk count indicates the number of clauses or provisions that may pose potential risks, allowing users to take proactive measures. The Law count reflects legal references within the contract, ensuring compliance and awareness of regulatory obligations. The Tax count highlights tax-related clauses, assisting in financial and statutory planning. To get these tags in Ai summary one must enable Ai in settings with proper credentials, allowing automated analysis to extract and present these essential details effectively.
