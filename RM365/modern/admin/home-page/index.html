Requisition: A requisition is a formal request to initiate a process or obtain approval for a specific need within an organization. It generally includes relevant details such as the purpose, associated department or function, required qualifications or criteria, and necessary approvals. This request is usually initiated by a responsible individual and must be authorized by designated authorities before further action is taken.

1. Click "New Requisition" to add a requisition.


2. The requisition form template includes two options. The first is a basic form, while the second provides additional fields such as salary details, work schedule, and more. Complete the requisition form and click the "Next" button to proceed.


3. The applicant form template offers three default versions. The first is a basic form, the second includes sections for education and personal details, and the third captures work experience and other relevant information. Fill out the applicant form and click the "Next" button to continue.


4. The final step in the process is to review the entered information and click the "Save" button to submit the requisition.


Job Posting: A job posting is a public advertisement for an open position within an organization. It is based on the approved requisition and provides prospective candidates with essential details such as job responsibilities, qualifications, and application procedures. Job postings are typically shared through various channels, including the company's career site, job boards, and social media platforms.


1. To create a new job posting, click on the "+ New Job Posting" button.


2. There are three main sections: Requisition, Applicant Form, and Hiring Process.

3. In the Requisition section, complete the form with details such as job title, description, required qualifications, and salary range. This helps ensure clarity and completeness in the job posting.


4. Click the "Next" button to proceed.


5. In the Applicant Form section, fields can be added using drag-and-drop. Applicants use this form to provide personal and professional details, such as name, contact information, education, work history, skills, and resume or cover letter uploads.


6. Click the "Next" button to continue.


7. In the Hiring Process section, link the job posting to a predefined hiring workflow. This includes steps such as application review, interviews, evaluations, and final offer issuance to ensure a consistent and organized process.


8. Click the "Edit" button to add a scorecard for candidate evaluation.


9. You can also add selectors by clicking on the "Edit" button.


10. After selecting the option, you can choose or import users to assign roles and responsibilities, then click "Save" to confirm.


11. After submitting, the job posting will appear in the Job Posting section. You can view it in the internal portal.


12. By clicking on the job title, you can view the list of candidates who have applied.



13. To schedule an interview, click the "Schedule Interview" button and complete the required details.


Applicant Detail List

14. The applicant detail list includes the name, email, job status, and phone number, along with additional information. You can also view the progress through various stages, such as screening, interviews, and the final round.


15. Click on the Action button to access multiple options such as adding new candidates, downloading candidate data, toggling view modes, or modifying visible columns in the list.


16. When you click on Add Candidate, a form will appear where you can enter candidate details and upload a resume. If Azure AI integration is enabled in the settings, the system will automatically fetch details from the uploaded resume.


Interview: An interview is a formal meeting between a job applicant and one or more representatives of the hiring organization. Its purpose is to evaluate the candidate’s qualifications, experience, and overall fit for the role. Interviews may take different formats, such as phone, video, or in-person, and often consist of multiple stages.

My Action: The Action tab provides an overview of candidates who have applied and progressed to the next step. From here, authorized users can take various hiring-related actions such as providing feedback, scheduling or conducting interviews, advancing candidates through the process, approving or rejecting applications, and managing other key steps in the recruitment workflow.
