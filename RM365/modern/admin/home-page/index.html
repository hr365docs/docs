    Requisition

    Create and manage job requisitions with ease. Define position details, requirements, and approval workflows to ensure efficient hiring requests.


    In the Requisition section, you will find both the All Approvals tab and the Draft tab. To learn more about the Draft tab and its functionality, please visit Draft Guide .


    Click New Requisition to create and submit a new hiring request.



    RM365 offers two default requisition form templates: a basic form and an extended form with additional sections such as compensation details, work schedule, and more. You can also create custom templates through the Custom Form option available in the settings panel. For detailed steps, see our Custom Form guide.



    When creating a requisition, you can select approver flow. If an approver flow is selected, the requisition will move to the job posting stage once it has been approved by the designated approver. If no approver flow is selected, the requisition will proceed directly to the job posting. To learn how to create an approver workflow, see our Approver Workflow guide.


    To hide any field from the job posting, click the eye icon as shown below.



    You can choose a template for the job description or write it manually. Once completed, click Next to proceed. You can also create custom templates through the Job Description Template option available in the settings panel. For detailed steps, see our Job Description Template guide.


The Applicant Form section offers three predefined templates: a basic version, one with educational and personal details, and a comprehensive version that includes work experience and additional sections.

You can also create custom templates through the Custom Form option available in the settings panel. For detailed instructions, refer to our Custom Form guide.



Use the Create Section button to add a new section and organize fields logically.



Add a custom field by clicking + New Field, entering the required details, and clicking Save.



For each field, you can edit the field name, add comments, mark it as mandatory using the checkbox, or delete it if no longer needed.



From the right-hand panel, drag any field into the relevant section of the form.



Select and complete the appropriate form, then click Next to proceed.


The hiring process in RM365 allows you to configure stages, scorecards, and evaluation questions to streamline recruitment workflows.


You can select a hiring process flow template. By default, there are two templates available: Three Section â€“ Three Stage and Three Section â€“ Two Stage. You can also create additional templates from Hiring process in setting panel. To learn more, refer to our Hiring Process guide.



Click + Add Stage under the Screening section to insert an additional stage in the screening process.



Click + Add Stage under the Interview section to define more interview stages as required.



Click the Import Scorecard button to import predefined scorecards for each stage.





Within the scorecard section, click Add to choose how you want to include evaluation questions:

  • Add Question: Allows you to define individual questions as needed.
  • Add Bulk Questions: Enables you to upload multiple questions in one action.


Each question includes configurable attributes such as category, complexity, access level, and relevant associations.



After completing the hiring process configuration, click the Submit button to finalize and save the requisition.



Job Posting

Publish job postings to internal and external platforms directly from RM365. Integrate with company portals and popular job boards for maximum reach.

The tabs allow you to view job postings categorized by their status, including Live, Paused, Closed, Archived, Upcoming, and Expired.



To create a new job posting, click on the + New Job Posting button.



There are three sections: Requisition, Applicant Form, and Hiring Process.


In the Requisition section, complete the form by providing the job title, description, qualifications, salary range, and other relevant information to create a clear and detailed posting.



Select the Publish Status to define where you want the job to be published. Options include Confidential External, Confidential Internal, External, and Internal. For a detailed guide, visit the portal configuration documentation .



Click on the Next button to proceed.



In the Applicant Form section, create sections by dragging the required fields. Candidates can provide personal and professional details, including education, work experience, skills, and upload a resume and cover letter.



Click on the Next button to proceed.



Map the job posting to a Hiring Process. This outlines recruitment stages, from application review to interviews, offers, and onboarding, ensuring a structured workflow.



When you click the Edit icon, you can modify the name of a stage (e.g., rename Screening 1 to a custom stage name) to better align with your recruitment process.




By selecting the Add Scorecard option, you can choose specific evaluation questions for that stage, ensuring structured and consistent candidate assessments.




When you select the Add Selector option, you can assign the stage to specific team members responsible for reviewing candidates at that stage.





Click Submit to finalize and publish the job posting.


The posting will now be visible in the Job Posting section and on the internal portal.



Click the three-dot menu in the job posting to access the following features:



1. Update Job Status â€“ Allows you to pause, close, or archive a job posting based on hiring needs.


2. Modify Job Post â€“ Enables you to edit the job details, such as title, description, and requirements.


3. View Job Applicants â€“ Displays a list of applicants for the selected job, along with their application status.


4. Assign Selectors â€“ Lets you assign specific selectors for each recruitment round, ensuring the right people are involved in candidate evaluation.


5. Create Delegations â€“ Grants selected users access to manage the job posting. Add the required users and click the Submit button to confirm the delegation.


Applicant Detail List

Click on the job title to view applicants for that position.



The Applicant Detail List displays each candidateâ€™s name, email, phone number, job status, and stage progress such as screening, interviews, and final round.



Click the Action menu to access various candidate management options, including adding candidates, downloading candidate data, switching view modes, and customizing visible columns.



1. Add Candidate: You can add a single candidate manually or upload multiple candidates in bulk using a file import option.



2. Download Candidate: Download candidate details either for the current stage only or for all stages in the recruitment process.



3. Toggle View Mode: You can view candidate info according to stage or section .



4. Modify Columns: In the Applicant Details section, choose which columns to display. After selecting the desired columns, click the Save button to apply the changes.


When adding a candidate, enter the required details and upload a resume. If Azure AI integration is enabled, the system will auto-extract details from the uploaded resume.



To schedule an interview, First click on applicant name then select Schedule Interview and complete the required details. You can view the Upcoming interviews in schedule interview tab.




Interviews

Schedule and manage interviews seamlessly with Outlook integration, ensuring smooth coordination and visibility for all stages.



As an administrator, you can view all scheduled interviews for monitoring and management.



Navigate through the Upcoming, Completed, Expired, and Cancelled tabs to quickly access interviews based on their current status.



Use the three-dot menu to mark it as completed, modify interview details or cancel an interview.



Click the calendar icon to view all scheduled interviews in a calendar format for better scheduling visibility.




My Action

Stay on top of your recruitment tasks with the My Action dashboard, showing pending approvals, interview feedback requests,moving the candidates and other key actions.


To provide feedback for a candidate, click on Give Feedback from the action menu.


In the feedback form, enter your remarks and select a decision option: Hire, No hire, or Hold. Once done, click Submit to save your feedback.


Additionally, you can view the candidateâ€™s details such as Candidate Info, Resume, Job Details, and Job Description before submitting your feedback.


Once you give feedback and decide whether to Hire or Not Hire, the next step is to move the candidate forward in the process. Go to the Move Candidate section, click on Move Candidate, provide the required feedback again, make your decision, and finally click Submit to update the candidateâ€™s status.



You can view the candidates for whom you are selected as an interviewer. Click on Take Interview, provide your remarks, make a decision (Hire, No Hire, or Hold), and then click Submit to record your remarks.



Once the candidate is hired, the Offer Letter generation option will become available in the applicant details, and it will also be visible under My Actions.


Click on genrate Offer to generate the offer letter.


Before generating the offer letter, you can navigate through the Candidate Info, Candidate Resume, Job Details, and Job Description tabs to review all relevant information.


You can create an Offer Letter Template by following the guide available at this documentation link .


Then, click on the highlighted icon to view the offer letter template.



Select an Approval Flow to define who must approve the offer before it is sent to the candidate. To add a new approval flow, refer to this guide: Approval Flow Setup.



Fill in all necessary details in the form, such as Acceptance Deadline, Annual Salary, Employment Start Date, and other job-related information. then click on Submit For approval to generate offer letter.
