    Features

    Helpdesk application features could be customized.

    Canned Responses

    Canned response settings are available for all admin, supervisor, and agents in the settings page. These responses can be viewed and used during a ticket raising in the comment page. To use these responses, click on the reply option and then on the canned response, the panel will open, select your response, and click on the add button.

    There are 4 types of canned response.

    Global canned response: These are public responses; all team members can create and use them freely.

    My canned response: These responses are individual or user specific, one who has created, can view and use during a ticket raising in the comment page.

    My signature: All team members can set their own default signature. The default signature comes up on the comment page during ticket raising. These responses or signatures are individual-specific.

    Meeting Invite: This option allows team members to quickly insert pre-defined meeting invite templates (e.g., with agenda, time, and conferencing details) into tickets or comments.


    To create the Canned Responses, follow the below steps:

    Applicable for global canned response, my canned response, my signature, and meeting invite.

    1. Go to the Settings, click Canned Responses under Features.

    2. Click +Add, this displays Add Canned Response.


    3. Enter Canned Response Title and Canned Message.

    4. Click on Save button.


    Delete: Click the delete icon, this displays a dialog box. Click Yes button.

    Edit: You can edit Title and Message Body. Click the edit icon, this displays Edit Canned Response. Update the Title and/or Message Body and click on Update button.

    Approver Workflow

    Use the Configure button to set up and manage approval workflows in Helpdesk 365.



    The Approval Workflow feature enables organizations to design structured approval processes for various requests and tickets. For instance, leave requests can automatically be routed to the respective manager for review and approval.

    Click + Add to create a new workflow and define its rules and conditions.



    1. Approval Workflow Based on Custom Forms

    When a custom form is selected from the dropdown and used to create a ticket, the ticket automatically follows the associated approval workflow. This ensures that form-based submissions are reviewed and approved in a systematic manner.

    Note: These workflows are triggered only during ticket creation. Status changes will not initiate them. After all approvers complete their actions, you may define a final step, such as assigning or closing the ticket.



    2. Approval Workflow Based on Teams and Services

    Workflows can be configured around teams, request types, services, and sub-services. When a ticket matches these conditions, the approval process is initiated. You can choose whether the workflow is triggered at ticket creation or during status updates.



    3. Approval Workflow Based on Custom Status

    This method applies to both new and existing tickets. When a ticket meets the defined criteria and its status is manually updated to a specified value, the approval workflow is triggered accordingly.



    Approval Levels and Approver Rules

    Each approval level offers two selection options:

    • All – Every approver at this level must approve before the workflow can proceed.
    • Any – Approval from a single approver at this level is sufficient to move the workflow forward.


    You can also set conditions to dynamically add approvers based on specific criteria, providing greater flexibility and control.



    The All, Any, and conditional rules are supported for both form-based and team/service-based workflows.

    After defining the approvers, you can set the final action for the workflow by choosing whether to Assign Ticket or Close Ticket. Once configured, click the Save button to apply your workflow.

    Time Tracking

    Admin, supervisor, agent and User can track time on their tickets. They could add more time during the comment of the tickets.



    Time tracking are disabled (on default), you could enable it by clicking Enable Time Tracking toggle switch. You may also enable Make Time Tracking Mandatory also.


    Users can select teams from the dropdown. Time tracking will be enabled only for the selected team, and the Track Time popup will not appear for tickets associated with other teams.



    To use the time tracking, follow the below steps:

    1. Go to the home page and click the Ticket ID to open the comment page.

    2. On the comment page, click the Reply button, this displays pop-up to write the comments.


    3. Click the Update button, this displays the Track Time popup.

    4. Enter the time taken to resolve the ticket in minutes.


      Here, user can see that the time tracking has started.

Enable comments in the ticket reply thread

When this feature is activated, replying to the requester includes the transmission of previous comments along with the response.

    User can also provide feedback on the published articles along with star ratings.
