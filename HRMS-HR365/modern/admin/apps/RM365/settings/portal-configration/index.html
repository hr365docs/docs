Portal Configuration

The portal configuration allows administrators to customize the external and internal job portal interface. Users can add a privacy policy link, configure portal buttons, update banner content, adjust text colors, and upload a logo that aligns with organizational branding.



Internal Job Portal Configuration


Set up and manage the internal job portal to streamline internal hiring workflows. This includes job template setup, access control, approval mechanisms, and tracking of employee job applications.



When configuring an internal job posting, follow these steps to ensure a complete and functional setup:

  1. Basic Details: Enter the title, footer, description, favicon icon, and portal logo. You can also upload two related images for branding purposes.


  2. Visual Customization: Add a banner image, select the portal color theme and text color, specify the company name, and provide the home page link along with its associated image.


  3. Internal Page Link: You must provide a valid internal page link. This is required to generate the live internal job posting site and its corresponding preview image.


  4. Hide SharePoint Default Components: To customize the portal view, click on Configure and enable the option to hide SharePoint’s default components.


  5. Referral Options: You can choose to hide or show the “Refer Candidate” / “Refer This Job” section by toggling the switch on or off.


  6. Finalize Setup: After entering all details, click on Save.


  7. Initially, the Live option will remain deactivated. Once you’ve added the required internal and home page links, the Live option will become active. Clicking this option will open the internal job posting site.



    8. Click on the provided icon to open the job application interface.


    Then, click the Apply button.


    Candidates can enter the required details per job criteria and upload a resume.


    The candidate details will be automatically extracted from the uploaded resume, eliminating the need for manual data entry.


    After completing the form, select the checkbox and click the submit button to finalize the application.

To activate the live option, ensure that the Internal Portal Configuration includes a valid internal page link and corresponding image. Once these are added, the live option becomes active, allowing you to click the link and view the job post with its associated image.


Confidential Internal


Confidential Internal jobs are shared only with selected employees via a private URL. These jobs do not appear in the standard internal job portal listings.


First, click on Internal Portal Configuration, enter the internal portal Home URL, and click Save to update the configuration.



After publishing a job as Confidential Internal, copy the unique job URL from the configuration. This private link can be shared directly with chosen employees, allowing access to only that specific job.




External Job Portal Configuration


All job postings made by the organization are publicly available on the external portal: RM365 Online Job Portal.



Confidential External


Confidential External jobs are accessible only through a private link and do not appear in the public external portal. This option is used when the job must remain discreet but still be available to selected external candidates.



Similar to Confidential Internal, after creating a job with Confidential External status, administrators can copy its URL and share it directly with chosen external candidates. Only individuals with the link can access and apply.




1. To install the Internal/External Job Portal (IJP), customers must raise a request with the support team. The support team will deploy and configure the IJP app on the customer’s SharePoint site, enabling administrators to set up and manage job postings.

2. For first-time RM365 installations within a tenant, once a user uploads a job posting and clicks the Live option, the system automatically opens the corresponding job posting site without requiring any additional configuration.
